What PVot is

PVot is a cloud based application that allows legally registered citizens of a country or members of a community to give online votes on issues of their concern. It can be used by governments or other administrative offices to collect opinions of their members or even to conduct national elections.

PVot embodies part of the working of an e-governmental system. Its role in reducing the immense money allotted for conducting elections is undeniable. On top of that, PVot enables users to vote from where they are without the difficulties of going to a polling station. Furthermore, it enables to see the cumulative results of a vote instantaneously, and this is a great cut in the time required to count votes manually before revealing the results.

Security has been a concern in the development of PVot. The django framework, on which PVot is based, has numerous internal security features. Some of those include immunity against SQL injection and cross-site request forgery.


How it works


In order to vote on PVot, one must be registered as a citizen or member of a community. When and how registration takes place is up to the discretion of the administration staff. Upon registration, citizens or members submit their full name and a unique voter id which they are ought to keep secret.

Upon voting, a user selects one among the available choices of the question and enters his contact information before hitting the vote button. The contact information consists of the first and last names of the voter as well as her unique voter id. A user can vote on a particular question only once.
